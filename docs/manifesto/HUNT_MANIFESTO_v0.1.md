# The HUNT Manifesto

**Version:** 0.1 Genesis  
**Project:** HUNT OS  
**Founder:** Toby Brandon  
**Status:** Living document

---

## 1. HUNT Is Not Just a Game

HUNT began as an upgraded zombie game concept, but its direction is much bigger.

HUNT is a modular live-action gaming platform.

It exists to help people create real-world adventures using reusable smart devices, downloadable scenarios and configurable game objects.

HUNT is not limited to zombies, laser tag, escape rooms, treasure hunts or scare attractions.

HUNT is the platform that can power all of them.

---

## 2. The Mission

To give creators the tools to build unforgettable real-world adventures without needing to design new electronics, write complex code or rebuild the system for every new game.

---

## 3. The Vision

A future where anyone can create immersive live-action games.

A scout leader can run a forest survival game.

A teacher can turn a history lesson into a Roman mission.

A family can create a treasure hunt in a park.

A haunted attraction can build a horror experience.

An escape room owner can add portable game objects.

A community can download and share new scenarios.

Different worlds.

Different stories.

One platform.

---

## 4. The Core Idea

Build the hardware once.

Create endless games with software.

A HUNT Base is not permanently a safe zone, generator, bomb, scanner or shop.

A HUNT Base is a configurable game object.

Its role comes from the selected scenario.

Today it may be a Safe Zone.

Tomorrow it may be a Treasure Chest.

Next week it may be a Magic Portal.

The hardware stays the same.

The story changes.

---

## 5. The Three Pillars

### Create

Creators should be able to build games without writing firmware from scratch.

### Play

Players should experience simple, exciting and memorable real-world adventures.

### Share

The community should eventually be able to share, download and sell scenarios.

---

## 6. The HUNT Promise

Every HUNT decision must improve at least one of these:

- Simplicity
- Creativity
- Reliability
- Replayability
- Community
- Commercial viability

If a feature does not support the platform, it should be questioned.

---

## 7. The HUNT Standard

A HUNT product should feel:

- Simple enough for beginners
- Powerful enough for professionals
- Flexible enough for many stories
- Reliable enough for paid public use
- Clear enough to operate without the creator standing nearby

---

## 8. The Hardware Principle

Hardware should have many lives.

New games should not require new core hardware.

If a new idea requires extra capability, it should be added through expansion modules where possible.

Expandable beats disposable.

---

## 9. The Software Principle

Games are scenario data.

The platform should avoid hard-coding one-off game logic when a reusable event, condition and action system can support many game types.

The scenario engine is the heart of HUNT.

---

## 10. The Community Principle

HUNT should eventually allow creators to build and share scenarios.

The long-term value of HUNT is not only the hardware.

It is the library of playable adventures that grows around it.

---

## 11. The Commercial Principle

HUNT should be designed as a real product from day one.

That means considering:

- Ease of use
- Manufacturing
- Cost
- Reliability
- Documentation
- Support
- Safety
- Packaging
- Updates
- Customer experience

A clever prototype is not enough.

HUNT must become something people can buy, understand and enjoy.

---

## 12. The Long-Term Goal

HUNT does not aim to become the biggest zombie game.

HUNT aims to become the most flexible live-action gaming platform.

The goal is for people to eventually say:

> Have you got HUNT? Let's play.

---

## 13. The Founder Standard

HUNT should always be built with imagination, discipline and honesty.

Big ideas are welcome.

Complexity must be challenged.

Good ideas should be documented.

Weak ideas should be improved or removed.

The platform matters more than any single feature.

---

## 14. Closing Statement

HUNT is not about electronics.

It is not about ESP32 boards.

It is not about one zombie game.

It is about giving people the power to create adventures together in the real world.

The hardware is only the beginning.

The story is the product.
